Participation Information Sheet

1. What is the research study about?

The research study aims to explore employees’ emotional displays at work and related attitudes and well-being. You are invited to take part in this research study because you have registered on Prolific, which is the platform via which this study is conducted.

2. Who are conducting this research?

The study is being carried out by the following researchers: Professor Xie at the Rotman School of Management, University of Toronto; Professor Lam at Alliance Manchester Business School, The University of Manchester; Professor Chen at College of Business, Law and Social Sciences, University of Derby; and Dr. Tang at Zhongnan University of Economics and Law. This research is being funded by a grant from Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada and Magna Professorship Grant from University of Toronto.

3. Do I have to take part in this research study?

Participation in this research study is entirely voluntary. If you do not want to take part, you do not have to. If you decide to take part in the research study, you will be asked to:

• Read the instruction and each question carefully; and

• Complete the online questionnaire (answer each of the questions).

4. What does participation in this research require, and are there any risks involved?

The questionnaire will take approximately XX minutes to complete. As a “thank you” for your time, you will be rewarded with £ X (English sterling pound if you are in UK) or $ X (if you are in Canada) for your participation in the survey. This amount of payment is according to the recommended pay rate on Prolific. Please note that the survey has attention check questions. Correctly answering the attention check questions is a precondition for you to receive the payment.

5. What are the possible benefits to participation?

We hope to use information we get from this research study to benefit others who read our research findings. We hope the readers will benefit from understanding what other employees are doing at work and their feelings about their work so that they can benchmark their own feelings and behaviors.

6. What will happen to information about me?

The research team will store the data collected from you and other participants for a minimum of 10 years after the publication of the research results. The information about you will be stored in a non-identifiable format where your individual identity will be kept unknown. The analysis will be based on aggregated data. The reported results will not identify any individual participants.

7. How and when will I find out what the results of the research study are?

The research team intends to publish the results of the research. All Information will be published in a way that will not identify you. If you would like to receive a copy of the results, you can reach out to the research team contact by sending them an email. Once the research is published, you will receive a copy of it.

8. What if I want to withdraw from the research study?

If you do consent to participate, you may withdraw at any time. You can do this by closing the questionnaire. If you withdraw from the research, we will destroy any information that has already been collected. You will not receive the payment if you withdraw from the survey. Once you have submitted the questionnaire, however, we will not be able to withdraw your responses as the questionnaire is anonymous.
